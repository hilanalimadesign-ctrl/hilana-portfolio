Overview

ScoreHub Digital Signature was designed to make digital signing processes more secure, intuitive and efficient by integrating identity verification, liveness detection, fraud prevention and document management into a single workflow.

Originally created within the ScoreHub ecosystem, the solution evolved into the platform’s primary product and is currently used by real customers in fraud-sensitive financial operations.

My contribution covered the entire design process, including discovery, user research, personas, journey mapping, prototyping, usability testing, mobile experience, web platform design and commercial communication through the product landing page.

01 • Context

Turning a highly sensitive process into a simple and trustworthy experience

Digitally signing credit contracts requires security, clarity and trust. At the same time, end users may have different levels of familiarity with technology, digital documents and biometric validation.

The challenge was to design a flow capable of preventing fraud without making the experience feel difficult, technical or intimidating for users who simply need to complete a signature.

The challenge

Balancing fraud prevention, security and usability for real users navigating financial processes.

02 • Discovery

Understanding user behavior, barriers and expectations

Before evolving the experience, we investigated users’ technological behavior, financial motivations, pain points and trust concerns throughout digital signing journeys.

Mapping user behavior, barriers, expectations and communication tone.

Personas created to guide accessibility, trust and communication decisions.

03 • User Journey

Mapping friction points before designing the interface

The journey was structured around moments of greater uncertainty: data confirmation, contract review, document submission, liveness detection, validation errors and signature completion.

User journey used to identify opportunities for simplification, clarity and security.

05 • Web Platform

Managing signatures in a single environment

Beyond the signer experience, the solution also needed to provide companies with visibility and control over contracts, statuses, validations, decisions and documents.

The web platform was designed to centralize process management, supporting transparency and operational monitoring throughout the signing journey.

Product decision

Clearly separating the end-user signing experience from the operational view used by companies, while maintaining consistency across both sides of the product.

Web interface for monitoring and managing digital signatures.

07 • Insights

What the data revealed

01

High cognitive load on the welcome screen

Users frequently interacted with non-clickable elements, indicating uncertainty about where to begin.

02

Weak affordance in document selection

Participants attempted to tap the text itself rather than the intended action area, revealing interaction ambiguity.

03

No clear next step after completion

After signing, users expected additional actions such as downloading a receipt or reviewing the signed contract.

08 • Iteration

Data-driven improvements

The experience was refined through behavioral analysis, usability testing and multiple design iterations focused on improving completion rates.

Refined prototype after usability testing.

Key improvements implemented

  • Larger touch targets
  • Stronger emphasis on primary actions
  • Loading feedback states
  • Improved hierarchy for legal information
  • Clear selection states
  • Post-signature actions
