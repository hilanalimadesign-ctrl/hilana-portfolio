Overview

ScoreHub is a B2B platform designed to support companies in credit granting and financial capacity assessment for both individuals and businesses.

The solution centralizes services such as SCR Inquiry, CPR Agenda, Score, Anti-fraud Signature and financial management into a single digital environment, reducing the need for multiple tools and making complex financial processes more accessible to users.

As the sole Product Designer on the project, I contributed from the initial concept of the platform to the design of flows, interfaces and components that shaped the product experience.

01 • Context

Transforming financial complexity into a simple product experience

Companies that perform credit analysis need to gather information from different sources, request authorizations, monitor inquiries and handle sensitive regulatory processes.

ScoreHub’s challenge was to create a platform capable of centralizing these operations into one experience without compromising security, clarity or scalability.

The main challenge

This was not just about designing screens, but structuring a modular platform capable of supporting different financial products with consistency, usability and room for growth.

05 • Main Flow

SCR Inquiry

SCR Inquiry was one of the core modules of the platform and became used by real clients, including GiroTech.

The flow needed to include legal representative authorization, base-date selection, status tracking and inquiry submission, keeping the process clear even with regulatory requirements.

Design decisions

I used visual hierarchy, contextual messages, tracking statuses and progressive information organization to reduce uncertainty throughout the process.

06 • Financial Data Management

CPR Agenda

The CPR module was designed to simplify the consultation and monitoring of registered receivables, requiring an interface capable of organizing large volumes of financial information in a scannable and efficient way.

07 • Financial Transparency

Financial Management & Platform Usage

Beyond financial inquiries, users also needed visibility into billing, service consumption and overall usage of contracted products.

The financial area was designed to present invoices, payment status, service consumption and visual indicators that help customers understand how they are using the platform.

Why does it matter?

In a B2B platform, financial transparency reduces uncertainty, improves predictability and helps customers better understand the value generated by the services they use.
